Earth

Diameter: 12,742 km

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Orbital Period: 365.25 days

Rotation Period: 23.9 hours

Orbital Mechanics

Earth orbits the Sun in an elliptical path, maintaining an average speed of 29.78 km/s. The planet's axial tilt of 23.5° causes our seasons as different parts receive varying amounts of sunlight throughout the year.

Moon

Diameter: 3,474 km

Distance from Earth: 384,400 km

Orbital Period: 27.3 days

Rotation Period: 27.3 days (tidally locked)

Orbital Mechanics

The Moon is tidally locked to Earth, meaning the same side always faces us. Its orbit causes tides on Earth through gravitational pull. The Moon's orbit is slightly elliptical, causing its apparent size to vary slightly when viewed from Earth.

Mars

Diameter: 6,779 km

Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km

Orbital Period: 687 Earth days

Rotation Period: 24.6 hours

Orbital Mechanics

Mars has a more eccentric orbit than Earth, meaning its distance from the Sun varies more significantly throughout its year. This contributes to Mars' extreme seasonal changes. The planet's orbit is also slightly inclined relative to Earth's.

Did You Know?

Mars has the largest dust storms in our solar system, sometimes covering the entire planet and lasting for months.

Orbital Mechanics

Objects in orbit follow Kepler's laws of planetary motion. The speed of an object in orbit varies - moving faster when closer to the central body (periapsis) and slower when farther away (apoapsis).
